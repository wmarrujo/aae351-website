Concept Development

Risk Assessment

Risks

  1. Ordinance explodes in vicinity of a person
  2. Ordinance explodes when a robot is clearing it
  3. Confirming an area clear without it being clear
  4. Excessive collateral damage from detonation
  5. Excessive pollution from non-detonation disposal
  6. Cost overrun
  7. Time overrun

Mitigation & Verification Strategies

  1. Deploy field personnel only when needed and when an area is deemed sufficiently safe-Follow OSHA procedures for training and safety equipment-Ensure any field personnel are qualified and experienced in UXO disposal.
  2. Only execute dangerous tasks like defusal when removal and neutralization are impractical-Make robots standardized and cheap to produce-Ensure robot pilots are qualified and experienced in UXO disposal-make robots durable to minimize damage.
  3. Combine multiple verification methods-Multiple checks and sweeps of the area - statistical analysis on these sweeps.
  4. Only commit to detonation when other methods are exhausted - ensure area is cleared properly-Use blast shields and containers to absorb blast from small explosives when possible.
  5. Ensure heavy metals and toxic compounds from defusal and disassembly are recycled when possible-Disposal of non-recoverable items with OSHA and EPA appropriate methods.
  6. Have a budget buffer - have regular budget checks-Raise awareness of the issue and attract extra funding from non-profit and corporate sponsors.
  7. Have a set schedule - have milestones and check with them regularly-commit extra personnel and resources when needed.

Cost and Value Assessment

According to the USDA, the average cost of farm land in Georgia is $3300/Acre. Assuming our technicians are paid $26 an hour, and 33% of our costs are overhead and maintenance, we must be able to clear land at less than 84 man-hour/acre. This appears to be feasible given sufficient autonomy of our robots. Due to the nature of the mission it is likely to attract funding from non-profit and defense contractors which will further increase sustainability and profitability.

Final Concept

The transport robots are the size of a full size pickup truck and with similar terrain accessibility. The disposal robots are the size of a forklift and armed with UXO disposal tools and detonation charges.

In a command center at a safe area, an experienced senior UXO disposal technician supervises a 10 trained technicians operating ordnance disposal robots. 20 disposal robots scan terrain in a 100-acre area autonomously with an internal metal detector while relaying visual data to the command center. Upon detection of an ordnance the robot stops and notifies a technician in the command center. The command center can also manually command the robot to stop if a point of interest is seen in the visual feed. The next available technician attempts to identify the ordinance based on visual data and measurements and historical data of the area. The technician then remotely pilots the robot to first attempt defusal, then attempt whole extraction, with the bomb or its disassembled components loaded on one of 10 autonomous transport robot that follows a fixed, isolated route to a site that will recycle those components or detonate the weapon in a secure container. All extracted parts and explosive byproducts are disposed based on EPA and OSHA guidelines. When the weapon cannot be safely extracted, the supervisor is called to authorize the use of an explosive charge to detonate the ordnance on site after retrieving all robots and evacuating personnel. After a disposal robot verifies the ordnance is detonated, the remaining disposal robots are returned to their original tasks. If the bomb has not been detonated the process is repeated with a larger charge.

After all bombs found in the initial sweep are removed the 100-acre area is scanned again to verify that all ordnance is removed. Based on the success rate of detection and the amount of ordnance found, the sweep is repeated until statistically the chance of munitions left behind is 0.1% or lower. The disposal robots then clean up any chemical residue from explosions.
