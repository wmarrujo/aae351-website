Initial Concepts

Concept Generation

Ordinance Discovery / Cleanup Verification Method

Solution 1 - Metal Detection

Since most ordinance comes in metal casings, a metal detection method could be used over the area to pinpoint the location of unexploded ordnance. This method would work best after a visual survey and would have the potential of seeing ordinance deep in the ground (within a meter or two) without disrupting the landscape.

With this solution new technologies could be invented to help aid in the location of ordinances. Some ideas would be metal detectors that can located ordinances deeper underground. Another type of technology could be some type of grid that could be set up over the whole area and help in the location of ordinances.

Solution 2 - Visual Inspection

A possible way to see the coverage of the ordinance could be visual inspection from the air. A drone search could be set up in which the covered areas are scanned for ordinance. The drones could find ordinances that are on the surface or just under the surface.

This would work best in open fields or generally uncluttered terrain. A fleet of drones is also an option. In addition, Machine learning and machine vision could be used to automate and thus cut down on the time and cost to perform the search.

Ordinance Removal

Solution 1 - Explosive Ordnance Disposal Unit

The way it is usually done for most unexploded ordnance currently is to send a team of trained professionals in to handle the bomb, and diffuse it, set it off in a controlled manner, or transport it. This is always a possibility, though it puts lives on the line.

Solution 2 - Robot

A versatile robot capable of doing the tasks of a human (save defusing the bomb) may be more practical. The robot will be expendable to a much higher degree than a human, but it does not have the same capabilities or manipulative capabilities when handling objects. Transportation of the robot and how it is controlled will be important aspects of the solution, as well as the investment made in each weighed with the cost of potential failure due to its constraints.

Ordinance Disposal

Solution 1 - On-site detonation

This is a common method for destroying explosive devices when the surrounding area is uninhabited and there is no immediate risk to surrounding environment. Since our target ordinances are in firing ranges with no buildings or residents around them, this is appropriate for any munitions that cannot be rendered inert or removed safely. This can be accomplished with a robot or human EOD operator placing a small explosive charge next to the UXO. When the UXO is removable or defusable those methods should be prioritized to minimize environmental damage.

Solution 2 - Transport to secure facility for disposal or storage

For devices that are unlikely to detonate during removal and handling, such as old degraded shells and landmines past the expiration date, it is possible to remove them and process them at specialized centers, that will either detonate them in a concentrated effort or strip useful and environmentally hazardous materials out.

Solution 3 - Chemical Neutralization

Certain explosives such as peroxides can be neutralized with metallic oxides. This allows removal, disassembly or ignoring the device. Other explosives can be neutralized or rendered less dangerous with other chemicals, but this is beyond the scope our class is equipped to explore.

Solution 4 - Defusion

Removing the detonation mechanism to ease transportation and disassembly. This is only possible with certain munitions such as AT mines or missiles.

Concept Selection

Of the options and concepts listed, the final procedure will involve using metal detection for the location of devices, robotic ordnance removal, and defusing explosives. The explosives that cannot be defused can be dealt with in other methods.
