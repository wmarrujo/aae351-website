Requirements Analysis

Stakeholder Analysis

U.S. Government
  • The government owns many of these ranges
  • The military (being a branch of the government) is responsible for many ranges
  • The government has a duty to clean up the land to keep its citizens safe
Community
  • Communities that have been plagued with unexploded ordnances have loss members of their communities every year. To help reduced the amounts of deaths that occur in their communities they need a way to remove these ordnances.
Anyone involved in doing the work of the cleanup
  • They will be directly involved, so the safety and efficiency of the process will affect them directly.
Environmental Protection Organizations
  • Unexploded ordnance usually has an environmental impact, both to the quality of the land, and to those who inhabit it
  • Both the action of cleaning them up poorly, and inaction (or late action) have the potential of having disastrous environmental consequences.
Defense contractors and equipment manufacturer
  • Defense contractors have fields where they test explosives, some of these sites have unexploded ordinances that need to be cleared.
  • They also would want their ordinances cleared in general from locations that are no longer war zones. So that random civilians aren’t killed giving them bad PR.

Requirements

No injuries or deaths resulting from cleanup efforts.

Since the entire point of the cleanup effort is to not have casualties, it would not make sense to design a system that allows for it. Measures should be taken to avoid casualties.

The area should be able to go back to its natural state after a maximum of 5 years

In many cases, there may be environmental impact from the unexploded ordnance and the goal here would be to have the area return to a natural, usable state within a short time so the community can begin using the space usefully again.

The community that may want to operate in that area should be able to operate within a year after the cleanup.

In cases where there isn’t much environmental damage, the goal should be to be able to get the community involved again in a short time.

There should be less than 1 incident every 50 years from the community using the area after the cleanup

This metric is useful in establishing how thorough our methods should be, since 100% clearance is near impossible, but the area must be safe enough to use.

Should fit within the EPAs discretionary budget (or designated budget for cleanup)

Since the EPA is in general the organization that funds these endeavors, the project should be optimized in such a way to make it feasible on the organization’s budget.

Cleanup cost must be lower than practical value of land and saved cost from military management

This requirement simply states the argument of whether it is worth it to perform the procedure on any given piece of land. The goal here is to make the cleanup cost low enough that it makes sense to perform the procedure.

Should be able to clean up the areas within 10 years

Based on the schedule included previously, a short time frame should be implemented to ensure safety quickly. Therefore, the solution should be designed with speed in mind too.

No laws or regulations are to be broken.
Statistical analysis that the area is clear to the requirements above.

This requirement states that there must be a way to ensure that the area is clear, since without one, how can we check on the status of the rest of the requirements. Some form of statistical analysis must be used because of the nature of our knowledge of unexploded ordinance on ranges.
